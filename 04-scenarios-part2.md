## E類：付款問題（Payment Issues）

### E-001
**場景**：客戶說「剛付款了」，但你們銀行還沒收到。
**你的回應**：
> "Thank you for letting us know. Could you please share the TT receipt (bank transfer slip/SWIFT confirmation) so we can follow up with our bank? 
> International transfers typically take 1-3 business days. We'll monitor and confirm receipt. If not received by [date], we'll advise you to contact your bank to trace the transfer."

---

### E-002
**場景**：客戶TT付款，銀行扣了手續費，到帳金額少了USD 45。
**策略**：明確付款條款應「full amount」，手續費由誰承擔需事先說明。
**你的回應**：
> "We've received your payment, but the net amount received is USD [X], which is USD 45 short of the invoice amount. 
> This is likely due to intermediate bank charges. Per our agreement, payment should be made in full (our quote was 'all bank charges outside China at buyer's account'). 
> Could you arrange a top-up of USD 45? Alternatively, we can deduct it from the next order if more convenient."

---

### E-003
**場景**：客戶說「財務部要到月底才能付款，現在付不了」——但你們的PI說裝船前付清。
**你的回應**：
> "I understand internal payment cycles can be constraining. However, our shipment is ready and the vessel is scheduled on [date]. 
> Options:
> 1. If you can arrange payment by [date - 2 days], we'll hold the booking
> 2. If payment will be delayed, we'll need to move to the next available sailing, which departs [next date]
> Please confirm so we can plan accordingly. Holding the booking beyond [date] may incur storage/demurrage fees."

---

### E-004
**場景**：客戶已經net 30付款期到了，但錢還沒到。你要如何催款？
**第一次催（到期當天）**：
> "Hi [Name], just a friendly reminder that Invoice [number] for USD [amount] was due on [date]. We'd appreciate your payment at your earliest convenience."

**第二次催（遲5天）**：
> "Following up on Invoice [number] — payment was due [date] and we have not yet received the funds. Could you please advise on the payment status? If there's an issue with the invoice, please let us know immediately."

**第三次催（遲14天）**：
> "We're concerned that Invoice [number] (USD [amount], due [date]) remains unpaid. This is now [X] days overdue. Please arrange payment within 3 business days, or we'll need to put future orders on hold until the account is cleared."

---

### E-005
**場景**：客戶說「公司財務出問題（financial difficulty），能否分期付款？」
**你的回應**：
> "I'm sorry to hear you're experiencing financial challenges. While our standard terms require full payment, we can discuss a structured payment plan for this invoice:
> Proposal: 40% now, 30% in 30 days, 30% in 60 days — with a written agreement.
> Before future orders, we'd also need to reassess credit terms. For now, please send us your proposed payment schedule in writing so we can present it to our finance team for approval."

---

### E-006
**場景**：客戶說信用狀（L/C）已開立，請你查看是否收到。
**操作步驟**：
> "Thank you. Please provide the L/C number, issuing bank name, and date of issue so we can follow up with our bank to confirm receipt of the SWIFT MT700 message. 
> Once received, our team will review the terms within 24 hours and revert if any amendments are needed."

---

### E-007
**場景**：信用狀到了，你發現交單期只有10天，這不夠用。
**你的回應**：
> "We've reviewed the L/C. We need to request an amendment — the presentation period of 10 days is insufficient for us to prepare all documents after shipment. 
> Please request your bank to amend: 'Presentation period to be 21 days after shipment date'
> Could you arrange this amendment within the next 3 days? We cannot proceed with production until the amended L/C is in place."

---

### E-008
**場景**：信用狀上的貨物描述（Description of Goods）比你PI上的多了一個字。
**策略**：這必須修改，不然單據不符。
**你的回應**：
> "Upon reviewing the L/C, I noticed the goods description reads '[L/C description]' while our PI states '[PI description].' Even a minor discrepancy can result in a documentary discrepancy. 
> Please arrange an amendment to align the description, or confirm which version is correct so we can adjust our commercial invoice accordingly."

---

### E-009
**場景**：信用狀到期日只剩2週，但客戶還沒確定能否改。
**你的回應**：
> "The L/C expiry date is [date] — that's only 14 days away, which is insufficient for production + shipment + document presentation. 
> We need a minimum extension to [new date] (covering [X]-day production + [X]-day transit + 21-day presentation).
> Please urgently request your bank to extend the expiry date. This is time-sensitive."

---

### E-010
**場景**：提單出了，但你發現提單上的目的港(Port of Discharge)與信用狀不符。
**策略**：這是嚴重的不符點！必須立刻處理。
**你的回應**：
> "URGENT: I've identified a discrepancy — the B/L shows POD as [wrong port] but the L/C requires [correct port]. 
> I'm working with our freight forwarder to have the B/L corrected (sea waybill amendment or telex release from carrier) before document presentation. 
> Could you simultaneously alert your bank that an amendment may be in process? I'll update you within 4 hours."

---

### E-011
**場景**：銀行退回你的信用狀單據，說有不符點（discrepancy）：發票金額與L/C金額相差USD 1。
**你的回應**：
> "We've received the bank's discrepancy notice — the $1 difference appears to be a rounding issue in our calculation. 
> Options:
> 1. We reissue the invoice with the exact L/C amount
> 2. You authorize the bank to accept documents 'as is' via cable authorization
> Which is faster for you? We want to minimize any delay in payment. Please advise as soon as possible."

---

### E-012
**場景**：貨物到了，但客戶說他沒有收到提單正本（Original B/L），無法清關。
**你的回應**：
> "Let me trace the original B/L shipment immediately. It was sent via [courier name] on [date], tracking number [XX]. 
> If the courier has delayed delivery:
> 1. We can request the carrier to issue a Telex Release (electronic release), allowing the consignee to pick up without the original B/L
> 2. Or we can arrange a new express courier at our cost
> I'll revert within 2 hours with the update."

---

### E-013
**場景**：客戶要求提前出貨，但L/C上的最早裝運日（Earliest Shipment Date）還沒到。
**你的回應**：
> "The L/C has an earliest shipment date of [date], so we cannot ship before that date under the L/C terms without risking a documentary discrepancy. 
> Options:
> 1. Request your bank to amend the earliest shipment date earlier
> 2. Ship on the earliest allowed date under the current L/C
> 3. If you need it truly urgently, we could ship on TT payment instead of L/C for this order
> Which approach works best?"

---

### E-014
**場景**：客戶說他們要把貨轉賣給第三方，問能否把提單收貨人（Consignee）填成對方公司。
**策略**：可以，但要確認L/C或付款方式沒有限制，同時需要知道為什麼（轉口貿易）。
**你的回應**：
> "We can issue the B/L with [Third Party Company] as consignee. Please confirm:
> 1. The full name and address of the consignee
> 2. Notify party information
> 3. Is there an L/C involved? (If so, the consignee must match L/C instructions)
> Please send the consignment details in writing for our records."

---

### E-015
**場景**：對方國家貨幣大幅貶值（如土耳其里拉、巴西雷亞爾），客戶要求用當地幣報價。
**策略**：避免外幣風險，堅持USD計價，或加入匯率調整條款。
**你的回應**：
> "We price all our exports in USD, as it's the global trade standard and protects both parties from currency volatility. 
> If your clients prefer local currency pricing, you may apply your own exchange rate when reselling. 
> Alternatively, we can include a currency adjustment clause: if the USD/[local currency] rate changes by more than 5% from the order date to payment date, we'll negotiate proportional adjustment. Would that work?"

## F類：物流與運輸（Logistics & Shipping）

### F-001
**場景**：客戶問能否指定他自己的貨代（nominated forwarder）。
**你的回應**：
> "Of course — if you have a preferred freight forwarder in China, we can work with them. Please provide their contact details (name, phone, email, and pickup address if different from port) and we'll coordinate directly. 
> For FOB terms, they'll arrange pickup from our factory/warehouse. For CIF/CFR, we'll compare rates between your forwarder and ours."

---

### F-002
**場景**：客戶問「能否空運（Air Freight）？」
**你的回應**：
> "Yes, air freight is available. For air, please note:
> - Chargeable weight is greater of actual weight or volumetric weight (L×W×H cm ÷ 6000)
> - Airport of departure: Guangzhou Baiyun (CAN) or Shenzhen (SZX)
> - Transit time: 5-7 days door-to-door
> - Cost is typically 5-8× higher than sea freight
> Would you like me to get a quote for your specific shipment volume/weight?"

---

### F-003
**場景**：貨物在運輸中損壞（transit damage），客戶提出保險索賠。
**你的回應**：
> "We're sorry to hear about the transit damage. To process an insurance claim:
> 1. Do NOT dispose of the damaged goods — they're needed as evidence
> 2. Take photos/videos immediately upon discovery
> 3. Note any external box damage and have the delivery driver note it on the waybill
> 4. File a cargo claim with the carrier/insurance company within [X] days (per policy)
> We'll provide the insurance certificate and commercial invoice for the claim. Let us know if you need assistance with the claim documentation."

---

### F-004
**場景**：客戶問能否做「door-to-door delivery」（工廠到門）。
**你的回應**：
> "Yes, we can arrange door-to-door delivery under DAP or DDP terms. To quote accurately, please provide:
> 1. Full delivery address with zip/postal code
> 2. Any specific unloading requirements (dock height, appointment needed?)
> 3. Does your facility handle LCL (pallet/loose cargo) or do you require FCL?
> We work with established freight partners in the US and Europe for door delivery."

---

### F-005
**場景**：客戶問貨物的CBM（體積）是多少，用來估算運費。
**你的回應**：
> "For your order of [X] cartons:
> Each carton: [L] × [W] × [H] cm
> Total volume: [X] CBM
> Total gross weight: [X] kgs
> This fits comfortably in a [20' / 40'] container, or approximately [X] pallets for LCL.
> Would you like me to get a freight quote from our forwarder?"

---

### F-006
**場景**：客戶要求「shipper's consolidation」——把你的貨和他在另一家工廠的貨拼到一個貨櫃。
**你的回應**：
> "We can coordinate with your other suppliers for a consolidation shipment. To arrange this:
> 1. Please provide the other supplier's name and address in China
> 2. Confirm which party will act as the consolidator (usually the freight forwarder)
> 3. Each supplier will need to confirm their ready dates
> Our forwarder can manage the consolidation if they're not already arranged. This can reduce your freight cost significantly."

---

### F-007
**場景**：貨物到了目的港，但客戶要求「免費倉儲（free storage）」超過30天，港口已開始收滯箱費（demurrage）。
**策略**：滯箱費由誰承擔取決於貿易條款和PI上的說明。
**你的回應**：
> "For FOB terms, once the goods are loaded on the vessel, risk and responsibility transfer to you. Demurrage/detention fees at the destination port are the consignee's (your) responsibility.
> I'd strongly recommend contacting your local freight agent immediately to arrange customs clearance and pickup. The per-day demurrage charge is approximately USD [X]/container.
> Is there anything we can do to help expedite the documentation on our end?"

---

### F-008
**場景**：貨物到了，但海關要求提供「Form E」（中國-東盟自由貿易協定原產地證），但廣澤之前沒有申請。
**你的回應**：
> "Unfortunately, Form E must be applied for before or at the time of shipment — it cannot be issued retroactively. 
> For this shipment, you'll need to pay the standard MFN duty rate.
> For all future shipments to [ASEAN country], please remind us to apply for Form E when you place the order. This can save you [X]% import duty. We'll add it to your account profile as a standard requirement."

---

### F-009
**場景**：客戶問能否拆分一個訂單分兩次出（partial shipment）。
**你的回應**：
> "Yes, we can arrange partial shipments. Please confirm:
> 1. Desired split quantity (e.g., 50% now, 50% in 30 days)
> 2. For L/C orders: does your L/C allow partial shipments? (Should state 'Partial shipments allowed')
> 3. Freight cost: each shipment will incur separate freight charges unless combined
> I'll arrange production and shipping according to your preferred split schedule."

---

### F-010
**場景**：貨物裝錯船了（wrong vessel），到了錯誤的港口。
**策略**：這是貨代/船公司的責任，你需要立刻行動。
**你的回應**：
> "This is a serious shipping error. I'm escalating this to our freight forwarder immediately. 
> We're investigating:
> 1. Which vessel the goods were actually loaded on
> 2. Actual arrival port and ETA
> 3. Options for re-routing to the correct destination
> I'll have an update within 2 hours. The freight forwarder will be responsible for any additional costs incurred. I'm extremely sorry for this disruption."

---

### F-011
**場景**：貨代通知說，目標港口因罷工(strike)，大量貨物積壓，建議轉改其他港口。
**你的回應**：
> "Noted. The port strike at [port] has created significant congestion. I'd like to discuss options before making any changes:
> 1. Alternative port: [alternative port] — what's the extra inland transportation cost for you?
> 2. Wait for strike resolution: ETA is uncertain but could be 1-3 weeks
> Please advise your preference. Also, if an L/C is involved, any port change will require an amendment — please alert your bank. 
> I'll hold the booking pending your instruction."

---

### F-012
**場景**：客戶要求「空運到貨後立即付款」，而平時是T/T 30/70（裝船前付70%）。
**策略**：空運更快，但付款方式不應改變。
**你的回應**：
> "We can ship by air freight as requested. However, our payment terms remain T/T 30% advance + 70% before shipment (i.e., before we arrange airway bill). 
> The air shipment will be arranged once balance payment is confirmed.
> Please process the remaining 70% and we'll book the first available flight."

---

### F-013
**場景**：客戶要求更改已在海上的貨物目的港（destination change）。
**你的回應**：
> "We can explore changing the destination through the carrier. Please note:
> 1. Cost: Diversion fees typically USD 500-2,000 + additional freight difference
> 2. Timing: Must be requested before the vessel arrives at the original POD
> 3. For L/C: This would create a discrepancy — the B/L POD would differ from the L/C
> I'm checking with the carrier now. What's the new destination and urgency?"

---

### F-014
**場景**：收貨方在目的港沒有取貨，貨物要被退運。
**策略**：退運成本高，先找原因。
**你的回應**：
> "I understand the goods are at risk of being returned or abandoned. Before taking action:
> 1. What is the reason the consignee cannot take delivery?
> 2. Can a local agent collect on their behalf?
> 3. Is there a chance of selling to a local buyer?
> Return shipping cost would be approximately USD [X]. If the order has been paid, we need to resolve this jointly. If unpaid, we may need to discuss legal options. Please advise urgently."

---

### F-015
**場景**：客戶收到的數量與提單不符（short shipment）。
**你的回應**：
> "We're very sorry for the quantity discrepancy. We're pulling our packing records and CCTV footage from the packing area to investigate. Could you:
> 1. Confirm the quantity received vs. invoiced quantity
> 2. Share photos of all cartons received (especially any damaged packaging)
> We'll determine if the shortage occurred at our end or in transit, and arrange makeup shipment or credit note accordingly."

## G類：客戶關係與特殊情況（Special Situations）

### G-001
**場景**：客戶說他們決定改用另一家供應商（competitor）。
**你的回應**：
> "I appreciate you letting me know. Before you finalize this decision, could you share what drove this change? We'd like to understand if there's anything we could have done differently.
> If it's pricing — we may have room to discuss.
> If it's quality or service — we'd genuinely want to address it.
> We value our relationship and hope to earn your business back in the future. May we stay in touch?"

---

### G-002
**場景**：客戶公司被收購了，新老闆說要重新審核所有供應商。
**你的回應**：
> "Congratulations on the transition! We'd welcome the opportunity to present our capabilities to the new management team.
> We've been supplying [products] to [company] for [X] years with a strong track record. 
> Could you introduce me to the relevant purchasing contact at the new parent company? We'd love to schedule a factory visit or virtual introduction to reaffirm our partnership."

---

### G-003
**場景**：客戶要求你簽NDA（保密協議），再才給你他的新產品設計圖。
**你的回應**：
> "Absolutely, we respect your IP and are happy to sign an NDA before receiving any proprietary information. Could you send us your standard NDA template?
> If you'd prefer to use ours, I can send Hirosawa's standard NDA for your legal team to review. Once signed by both parties, I'll request the design files immediately."

---

### G-004
**場景**：客戶邀請你去他們總部做年度供應商審核（Annual Supplier Review）。
**你的回應**：
> "We'd be delighted to participate in your annual supplier review. To prepare effectively, could you share:
> 1. Agenda / topics to be covered
> 2. KPIs you'll be evaluating (quality, delivery, cost, service)
> 3. Any pre-submission documents required
> Our team will prepare a comprehensive performance review and roadmap for the next 12 months. We see this as a valuable opportunity to strengthen our partnership."

---

### G-005
**場景**：客戶要求廣澤提供財務報表（financial statements），以評估供應商穩定性。
**策略**：財務報表是機密文件，可以提供部分資訊或讓客戶簽NDA後提供。
**你的回應**：
> "We understand the need to assess supplier financial stability. While we don't publicly disclose full financial statements, we can provide:
> 1. Bank reference letter from our primary bank
> 2. Dun & Bradstreet or similar credit report (we can arrange this)
> 3. General business overview (revenue range, capital structure)
> For comprehensive financial disclosure, we'd require an NDA. Would any of the above options meet your requirements?"

---

### G-006

**你的回應**：

> Raw material costs and exchange rates have moved since then. Let me check with our team and reconfirm the current pricing within 24 hours.
> We'll do our best to honor the original price — I'll revert shortly."

---

### G-007

## H類：價格談判（Price Negotiation）

### H-001
**場景**：客戶說「你的價格跟去年一樣，但我知道原材料降價了，應該便宜」。
**策略**：不要主動降，要解釋成本結構，尤其是非原料部分的漲幅。
**你的回應**：
> "While raw material prices (PP resin) have fluctuated, other cost components have increased:
> - Labor cost: +8% YoY
> - Energy cost: +12% YoY  
> - Logistics/packaging: +5% YoY
> The net effect is roughly neutral. That said, for larger volume commitments, we do have room to offer scale pricing. What volume increase are you considering?"

---

### H-002
**場景**：客戶說「你們的競爭對手比你便宜15%，你能match嗎？」
**你的回應**：
> "We'd love to match if we can. Could you share the competitor's quotation so we can do a fair comparison? We'd want to ensure:
> 1. Same material specification
> 2. Same surface finish standard
> 3. Same quality certification level
> 4. Same payment and delivery terms
> Often a 15% price gap reflects differences in specification or quality level. If it's a true apples-to-apples comparison, we'll do our best to close the gap."

---

### H-003
**場景**：你主動提出年度合約，承諾客戶固定價格12個月，換取客戶給你量的保證。
**你的回應**：
> "I'd like to propose a 12-month pricing agreement for your key items. In exchange for a committed annual volume, we can lock in today's price for the full year — protecting you from raw material price increases.
> Proposed structure:
> - You commit to [X,000] pcs/year minimum
> - We guarantee the price through [date]
> - Quarterly delivery schedule, with call-off flexibility ±20%
> Would this type of arrangement work for your planning process?"

---

### H-004
**場景**：客戶要求最低報價（bottom-out price），威脅說做不了就換人。
**策略**：不要被威脅拖著走。展示底氣，但也給台階。
**你的回應**：
> "I understand you need the best price possible, and we genuinely want to work with you. I can offer our absolute best price of USD [X.XX] — this reflects all available cost optimizations we can achieve at your current volume.
> Below this, we'd be manufacturing at a loss, which wouldn't be sustainable for either party in the long run.
> If you can confirm the order today, I can also offer [net 15 extra days payment terms / free sample shipment / other value add]. I hope we can find a way to keep this business together."

---

### H-005
**場景**：鋼材/塑料原料暴漲（如因能源危機），你需要通知客戶漲價。
**你的回應**：
> "I need to bring an important matter to your attention. PP resin prices have increased by [X]% since our last pricing was set, driven by [energy costs / supply chain disruption]. 
> We've absorbed this increase for [X] months, but it's no longer sustainable. We need to apply a price adjustment of [X]% effective for orders shipped after [date].
> We value our relationship and have delayed this conversation as long as possible. For orders already placed, we'll honor the original pricing. Please let me know if you'd like to discuss."

---

### H-006
**場景**：客戶說「我有3家供應商，你們給我最好的價就繼續合作，不然我給其他家」——典型的三角競標。
**你的回應**：
> "We appreciate the transparency about your sourcing strategy. Rather than simply providing a number, I'd like to understand more about what matters most to you — is it purely price, or is there value in our quality record, delivery reliability, or technical support?
> We'll submit our most competitive offer. But I'd also like to have a 20-minute conversation with you to understand your total cost of ownership, not just unit price. When works for you?"

---

### H-007
**場景**：客戶下了長期合約後要求「年度降價2%（Annual Cost Reduction，ACR）」。
**策略**：這在汽車行業是常見慣例（豐田、本田都這樣要求），要有準備。
**你的回應**：
> "Annual cost reduction is something we plan for proactively. We support a [1-2%] annual reduction driven by:
> - Production efficiency improvements (kaizen activities)
> - Material usage optimization
> - Tooling life extension
> We would need to jointly review volumes, design opportunities, and lead time expectations before committing to a specific reduction target. Can we schedule a joint cost reduction workshop for [month]?"

---

### H-008
**場景**：你即將結束一場談判，需要一個「closing statement」來促成訂單。
**你的回應**：
> "I appreciate the productive discussion today. Based on everything we've covered:
> - Price: USD [X.XX] FOB Yantian
> - Quantity: [X] pcs
> - Lead time: [X] weeks
> - Payment: T/T 30%/70%
> I'll send a revised PI reflecting today's agreed terms. If you can confirm by [date], I can lock in our production schedule for [month] — which is our peak availability window. 
> Looking forward to your confirmation."

---

### H-009
**場景**：你報價後客戶沉默了很長時間，然後突然說「可以接受，但要送300個樣品」。
**策略**：「免費」的要求都要評估——樣品是有成本的。
**你的回應**：
> "Thank you for the confirmation. Regarding the 300 samples: at our standard sample price of USD [X] each, that's USD [total]. 
> We can offer: first 50 pcs free as our standard sample policy. For the remaining 250 pcs, we can offer at 50% of the production price.
> Alternatively, if you place the bulk order today, we can include the 300 samples at no extra charge as part of the first production run. Would that work?"

---

### H-010
**場景**：客戶說「先讓我做市場測試，如果賣得好我就大量訂」——要求極低的試單。
**你的回應**：
> "Market testing makes sense, and we want to support your success. For a trial order:
> - Minimum trial quantity: [X] pcs at [trial price + X% small-order surcharge]
> - If follow-up order exceeds [Y] pcs within 3 months, we'll issue a retroactive rebate to bring the per-unit cost down to standard bulk pricing
> This way you have low initial investment, and we have incentive alignment for success. Shall I prepare a trial order quotation?"

## I類：信用狀高頻情境（L/C Advanced Scenarios）

### I-001
**場景**：信用狀上有一條「貨物須由船公司直航（No Transshipment）」，但航線需要轉船。
**你的回應**：
> "The L/C prohibits transshipment, but our available routing to [destination] requires transshipment at [port]. We need to:
> Option 1: Request L/C amendment to 'Transshipment Allowed'
> Option 2: Find a direct sailing — please note this may extend transit by [X] days and increase freight cost
> Could you ask your bank/buyer to amend the transshipment clause? Direct sailing option: available on [date] from [port], arriving [date]."

---

### I-002
**場景**：信用狀金額是「USD 50,000 exact」，但你的實際發票是USD 49,876。
**你的回應**：
> "Our actual invoice amount is USD 49,876 which is below the L/C face value of USD 50,000. This is acceptable — you can draw under the L/C for the actual invoice amount. No amendment needed.
> However, please confirm: does the L/C allow partial drawings? (Check for 'partial shipments not allowed' — this could also restrict partial drawing). I'll verify our L/C terms."

---

### I-003
**場景**：你需要向客戶解釋什麼是「不符點費（discrepancy fee）」。
**你的回應**：
> "A discrepancy fee is charged by the bank when they find errors or inconsistencies in the documents submitted under an L/C. Common discrepancies include: invoice description doesn't match L/C wording, late presentation, wrong B/L consignee, etc.
> The fee is typically USD 75-150 per discrepancy and is usually deducted from the payment to the seller.
> To avoid this, I always review the L/C carefully before shipment and check all documents against the L/C requirements before submission. Prevention is much better than fixing discrepancies after the fact."

---

### I-004
**場景**：客戶說他們的銀行不熟悉中國業務，問你能推薦哪些銀行開L/C。
**你的回應**：
> "For international L/C transactions with China, we recommend working with banks that have strong correspondent banking relationships with Chinese banks. Common choices:
> - Large international banks: HSBC, Citibank, Standard Chartered, Deutsche Bank
> - Local banks with good China connectivity: Wells Fargo, Bank of America (for US clients)
> - Our preferred corresponding bank in China is [Bank of China / ICBC / HSBC China]
> Having a well-known issuing bank also speeds up the negotiation process on our end."

---

### I-005
**場景**：客戶問「保兌信用狀（Confirmed L/C）和普通信用狀有什麼差別？要多收費嗎？」
**你的回應**：
> "A confirmed L/C adds a guarantee from a local/international bank in addition to the issuing bank. This means even if the issuing bank (in the buyer's country) defaults, the confirming bank will still pay us.
> For buyers in countries with higher political or banking risk, we may require a confirmed L/C. The confirmation fee is typically 0.1-0.3% of the L/C value, charged by the confirming bank.
> For your country/bank, [we require / we don't require] a confirmed L/C. Would you like more details?"
